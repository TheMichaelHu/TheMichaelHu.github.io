About Me

Hello! My name is Michael Hu and I'm a second year Computer Science major at Northeastern University. I'm still trying to decide what I want to do with my life, so right now the plan is to just do a lot of really awesome things until something clicks.

When not working on software, I tend to dabble in graphic design, video production, photography, tabletop gaming, ultimate frisbee, and adventuring into the wilderness.

The whole #!(sh-bang)

I was born in Ames, IA but grew up in the cold, dark forests of southern New Hampshire. Like any other self-respecting nerd-child, I grew up loving Pokemon, MMORPGs, and robots. That last one is actually what got me into programming; I started out in FIRST Lego League and, by 9th grade, found myself learning C++ for FIRST robotics. I guess one thing lead to another and now I'm a computer science major.

I'm currently a second year student at Northeastern University learning how to subsist without ever ungluing my eyes from my computer screen, or so I'd like people to think. In actuality, I'm quite active on the student organization scene and I often find the time to ditch my technology for a couple days and go hiking or camping or whatnot. I'm also an amateur film maker and amateur amateur photographer (read: guy with a decent camera and functional index finger).

Contradicting my previous assertion and going back to my orginal statement, I do spend an unhealthy amount of time around computers. I program computers, I fix computers, I even took a test on computers once and got a fancy paper out of it! Of course, some good things have come out of my technophilic lifestyle, namely a lot of terrible coding puns and fodder for my projects page.
